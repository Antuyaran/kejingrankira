My first project wanted to discuss the issue of public space surveillance in China, and projects two and three both worked on the User Privacy Treaty. In fact, the User Privacy Treaty is also a cyber extension of public space surveillance, except that the government has ceded the authority to manage users to online companies.
Users share their information with online companies in order to have a healthy and friendly cyber public space, just as we hand over our registration information, identification numbers and many other private information to state regulators in order to have a safe living environment by means of surveillance. Examples: underground security checks, etc. (But it is questionable whether we will be able to live in the environment we want after giving up our privacy)
The fact that so much personal information is widely read and reviewed by online companies will inevitably lead to the misuse of personal information, and those who do not want to be read will be excluded from the environment as a hidden danger.
In the midst of a surveillance society, we may not have a choice between ceding our personal privacy and withdrawing from contemporary life in China.
