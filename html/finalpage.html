Interstice Living

"Interstice Living" is a small zine that explores the issues of young mobile populations, home, and space. In the book, I chose a dilapidated residential building in the center of Shanghai, which is scheduled for demolition, as the protagonist's dwelling. I created a fictional character named Qi Yun, a young migrant who resides in this abandoned and perilous building without water and electricity. The content of the book revolves around her life and reflections within this space.
The juxtaposition of the urban and the ruins, the natives and the migrants, in a space forged by steel and iron, constantly generates friction and collisions of diverse needs, akin to planets passing each other in the universe. In the accidental gaps created by the friction between two colossal stones, dust and debris continue to fall. Qi Yun is the person living amidst these fragments.
